Project Specification

To undertake the role of Media and Communications Director for the first women in technology society at the University of Queensland, an institution over a century old. To help create presence and recognition of the society starting its first year in the midst of a global pandemic, where face to face interaction with students and sponsors is not possible.

The Role of Marketing

To be successful in its first year and continue on, the society must have a certain number of members attend the annual general meeting at the end of the year. More members allow for more funding for events hosted for members whether it be social or professional network events. Additionally, funding from potential sponsorships allows the society to host initiatives such as mentorship programs and career informational sessions. Therefore marketing of the society is crucial in helping the society becoming established and achieving its goals during the first year.

Where It Started

Joining the founding members in February 2020 as an Inaugural executive, the society had already defined its primary logo, colour and font. They chose a light pink as a nod to the femininity of the society, a light bulb to signify our acronym "LIT" - for Ladies in Technology and a timeless serif font to tie it all together. From these given materials, the mission was to create a recognisable and professional aesthetic that appeals to both students and potential sponsors.

Inspirations

The purpose of LIT was to uplift, inspire and create a supportive community for the women undertaking technology disciplines at UQ. The society wanted to use its platform to bring like minded people together and help educate and assist them in achieving their goals. We took aesthetic and content inspirations from organisations that had similar initiatives such as @bosswomancollective, who used infographical content to engage their followers.

Developing Recognition

In order to facilitate recognition for the society, a recognisable aesthetic was constructed through the use of chosen fonts, colours and graphics in the societies social media posts and marketing material. The initial colour additions were a washed out pink, the society's official pink and navy. After using these colours for a short period of time, we determined that the colours made the content feel limited and repetitive and did not communicate to our audience our fresh and exciting message. To remedy this, brighter and diverse colours were added to give our content a brighter, positive feel. The colours that we added were orange, light orange, lilac and a dark pink.

Educating, Inspiring Content

Due to the society being unable to interact with its key stakeholders face to face, creating content that helped engage our followers was crucial. We set out a marketing plan and initiative for every semester, which planned and scheduled content to be posted. As well as creating content for our upcoming events, we produced educative content relevant to the tech space and the student experience to help inspire and engage potential members with the society. The visual representation of these posts were also considered in its feed form and posts were specifically designed based on the posts around them, so as to give a sense of consistency and professionalism to the user scrolling through the page.

Creating a Lasting Impression

To diversify our posts and distinguish between our infographical content and official society event content, I created a secondary logo for the society using Adobe Illustrator. The logo incorporates our signature lightbulb with the words "lit" as the filament. This was added to give personality to our posts and cement our branding. Eventually the lightbulb became a recurring theme in our marketing, as we incorporated lightbulb graphics in some backgrounds too.

Final Product

As the Media and Communications Director, I lead and organised a team of sub executives to successfully achieve our marketing goals and initiatives. Throughout the year we received a steady increase in interaction from our followers, gained new members and became a recognisable society within the university. Our marketing aesthetic even received recognition from the university's engineering and technology faculty's social media!
