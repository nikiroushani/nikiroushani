Project Specification

Final project for Introduction to Web Design (DECO1400), awarded a high distinction.


Assignment Brief: "You will design and implement a themed resort website, which combines an unusual location and activity of your choosing in an engaging and interactive manner".


Produce a final report describing the design and development decisions for the website as they relate to the brief. Design & development choices are to be justified with relevance to research, design principles and good practice web design. Develop the website using HTML and CSS. The website should have at least 4 unique, meaningful interactions implemented with JavaScript.

Website Topic Introduction

The website topic is based around a fictional island called “Vinland”. Vinland is located on the outskirts of Italy and was founded by Captain (Cap) Sauvignon in 1880. When Cap Sauvignon arrived on the island, he described it as a natural phenomenon, as he found mountains and an abundance of land covered in vineyards. As he moved closer into the island, he found naturally occurring wine lakes and prosecco waterfalls.

Defining Audience

User Personas were used to define and understand the audience that may use the website. Generally, the island is intended for a target audience that is over the age of 18 and for people who have an interest in or enjoy wine.

Determining Navigation and Organisation

To determine the appropriate navigation and organisation system for the website, a card sort user testing was conducted.


The initial testing plan was designed to have pages sorted under contents and actions. The users were then asked to conduct an open card sort where they create their own headings that they believe appropriate for the grouping of pages. Site Map and Content Structure

After the navigation and organisation of the site was determined through user testing, the site map and content structure of the website was determined and visualised using a flow chart site map.

Visual Organisation

It was determined that the website would have a theme of a hero slide background, with the initial slide having the page divided in thirds gird spacing so as to help distinguish three different action points - the logo, navigation menu and the book button. Subsequent informational pages will have a fade in scroll slide show effect, to allow separation of topics and ideas.


This flow of information will help engage the target audience as it requires them to interact and scroll through bitesize amounts of information instead of being overwhelmed with large chunks of text. This gradual release of information will prompt them to explore the website more and absorb the information with interest.
