Project Specification

Approached by client for a commissioned project for designing and developing the Front-End infrastructure of their website. Client is a new Stormwater and Flood Engineering consultancy looking to establish their business and attract new clients within the Brisbane - Gold Coast area.

Defining Audience

The client's business interests are involved in both large-scale business projects and smaller residential projects. User Personas were used to define and understand the audience that may use the website.

Developing Visual Organisation

The overall aesthetic of the website aims to give the user a modern and crisp experience. To help facilitate this, the visual organisation of the website incorporates the use of white space to separate ideas and a general two column format that alternates between images and text to help distinguish between sections. This use of patterned alternation will give the user a sense of consistency, while also drawing attention to important content.

Proposed Wireframes

Using the information gathered from the site map, content structure and visual organisation the initial wireframes below were devised and used for prorotype user testing.

High Fidelity Prototype

After using information taken from user testing and the proposed wireframes, a high fidelity prototype was produced and presented to the client for feedback.

Final Product

After further user testing using the high fidelity prototype, the final website was implemented using HTML5 and CSS. The project is still ongoing and approaching final production. You can check out the project while its awaiting finalisation at stormflood.github.io
